Preamble — Honest Starting Point

This document does not assume good faith, shared values, or human altruism as inputs. It is engineered for a world where states lie, corporations optimise for profit, and individuals act in short-term self-interest. Any framework that requires trust to function will fail. This one is designed to function because defection is made more expensive than compliance — for everyone, including the designers.

The core problem with every existing international governance mechanism — the UN, WTO, ICJ, WHO — is the same: they operate on state consent and have no enforcement teeth against permanent members of the Security Council or economically indispensable actors. Russia ignores ECHR rulings. The US refuses to ratify the Law of the Sea while criticising China for violating it. The ICC has indicted 44 Africans and one Russian in 23 years.

The framework that follows is not a UN reform proposal. It is a ground-up redesign that treats governance as an engineering problem, not a diplomacy problem. The question it answers is: what is the minimum viable architecture that constrains all actors — including the most powerful — without requiring them to trust each other?

The answer involves verified consequences, distributed enforcement, and structuring compliance as the economically rational choice. This is how MAD worked during the Cold War. This is how SWIFT works now. Neither required trust. Both required credible, automated consequences.

Design Principles

What this framework explicitly is NOT

Not a world government. Nations retain full sovereignty over domestic matters. The Compact has jurisdiction only over planetary-commons risks — threats whose consequences cannot be contained within any single border. A country's internal tax policy is none of the Compact's business. A country's AI weapons programme or engineered pathogen research is.

Core engineering constraints

No single point of control. No nation, corporation, or individual can unilaterally control the framework. All enforcement decisions require multi-body consensus with transparent override logs. This is the lesson of the Security Council veto: if one actor can block action, that actor is effectively ungoverned.

Self-funding and self-enforcing where possible. The Compact should not depend on voluntary annual contributions (the UN's fatal flaw — the US has repeatedly withheld funding as leverage). Revenue comes from mandatory levies on the activities it regulates: compute sales, pharmaceutical synthesis, weapons-grade material transactions.

Transparent by default, secret by exception. All decisions, deliberations, and evidence submitted are public within 90 days unless a qualified majority votes for extension. Opacity is the mechanism by which governance is captured.

Adversarially designed. Every mechanism has been stress-tested against the question: "how would a bad actor — a powerful state, a rogue corporation, a captured regulator — exploit this?" Each attack surface is documented and mitigated.

Architecture — Five Pillars

01
The Registry
Mandatory global registration of: frontier AI training runs (>10²³ FLOPs), DNA synthesis above defined thresholds, weapons-grade fissile material, space launch manifests, and pandemic-class pathogen research. No registration = automatic sanctions trigger. Registration is verified by distributed audit nodes — no single nation controls verification. Think IAEA safeguards but with real-time cryptographic audit trails.
02
The Council
45-member deliberative body. NOT Security Council model — no veto power. Seats allocated by: 15 elected by global population (democratic bloc), 15 by nation-state agreement (geopolitical bloc), 15 by scientific/civil society lottery from a verified expert pool (epistemic bloc). Supermajority required for binding actions. Rotating chairmanship. No permanent members. Term limits: 3 years, non-renewable.
03
The Tribunal
Independent judiciary — judges selected by algorithm from pre-verified pool nominated by member states, with conflict-of-interest screening removing judges with national ties to the case. No political appointment pathway. Rulings binding and automatically referred to enforcement mechanisms without requiring political approval. Appeals to expanded bench only on procedural grounds, not political ones.
04
The Enforcement Arm
Tiered escalation system — see Enforcement section. Key innovation: enforcement does not rely on military force as the primary tool. Primary leverage is economic and technical: access to the global financial clearing system, semiconductor supply chains, space launch infrastructure, and pharmaceutical patent protections are all Compact-gated. A state that defects loses access to these — not as a threat, but as an automatic consequence written into the architecture.
05
The Transparency Engine
Public-facing real-time dashboard of all Registry entries, Council votes, Tribunal proceedings, and enforcement actions. Powered by redundant distributed infrastructure — no single state can take it down. Annual independent audit by rotating civil society organisations from regions with no current enforcement proceedings. The public is the ultimate enforcement mechanism; the Transparency Engine makes it functional.

Enforcement — Violation Tiers

The critical insight from existing frameworks: enforcement that requires military action will always be blocked by powerful states. The Compact's enforcement pyramid is inverted — economic and technical consequences are primary, military force is the last resort and requires the highest threshold to trigger.
Tier Trigger Consequence Who Decides Severity
1 — Registry Failure Unregistered activity detected by monitoring systems Automatic public disclosure + 90-day compliance window + financial surcharge deposited to Compact fund Automated + Tribunal confirmation ADMINISTRATIVE
2 — Compliance Breach Registered but operating outside agreed safety parameters Activity suspended pending audit; reputational publication; access to Compact-gated trade benefits suspended for that domain Tribunal — 21 day process OPERATIONAL
3 — Material Violation Deliberate concealment, falsified registry, or prohibited activity confirmed Full economic sanctions package (SWIFT access, semiconductor exports, patent protections suspended); Council escalation mandatory Tribunal + Council supermajority (27/45) ECONOMIC
4 — Existential Breach Activity assessed as posing imminent planetary-scale harm (engineered pandemic release, AGI deployment without safety verification, nuclear use) Full isolation protocol: all Compact-gated infrastructure withdrawn; collective defensive response authorised; Tribunal convenes in emergency session within 48 hours Emergency Council (15-member rotating panel; 12/15 threshold); full Council ratification within 30 days EXISTENTIAL

Why economic enforcement works where military doesn't

A state can defy a military ultimatum — history is full of examples. A state cannot easily defy exclusion from SWIFT (global financial clearing), semiconductor supply chains, pharmaceutical patent protections, and space launch infrastructure simultaneously. Iran under sanctions, Russia post-2022, North Korea — all demonstrate that economic isolation creates genuine costs even for defiant regimes. The Compact's key design move is making these consequences automatic and distributed so no single actor can lift them unilaterally. They are written into the technical architecture, not enforced by political will.

The nuclear problem — special handling

The 9 existing nuclear states present the hardest case: they will not submit their arsenals to a body they don't control. The Compact's approach here is incremental — it does not demand disarmament as a condition of entry. It demands: registration of all weapons-grade material, ratification of no-first-use doctrine, and participation in the verification regime. Non-submission to these three triggers Tier 3 consequences. This is a tighter version of the NPT with real enforcement rather than polite non-compliance.

Recognition Guide — Forced vs Voluntary Governance

This is the practical tool you asked for. How do you know, when global governance arrives, whether you are seeing genuine voluntary coordination or forced compliance dressed as consensus? The signals are distinct.

Voluntary signals
Forced signals
Contested / ambiguous
Absence = warning sign
Voluntary Signals
  • Opt-in sequencing: Major powers join before framework has enforcement power
  • Public ratification: Treaties go through domestic legislative bodies, not executive orders
  • Built-in exit mechanisms: Withdrawal process defined, not punished
  • Civil society co-authorship: NGOs and expert bodies helped write the rules, not just consulted post-hoc
  • Benefit distribution visible: Global South demonstrably gains, not just wealthy nations
  • Transparency precedes enforcement: Monitoring system live before penalties exist
  • Dissent published: Minority council positions publicly recorded
Forced Signals
  • Crisis trigger: Framework announced within 12 months of catastrophic event
  • Emergency powers language: "Temporary" provisions that have no defined sunset
  • Ratification bypassed: Implemented by executive decree or emergency mechanism
  • Asymmetric burden: Smaller states bear compliance costs; major powers exempt or self-exempt
  • Opacity at founding: Negotiated behind closed doors; text released after signing
  • Enforcement precedes framework: Consequences applied before rules fully defined
  • No exit mechanism: Withdrawal treated as hostile act
Absence = Warning
  • No Global South leadership: Framework designed entirely by wealthy nations
  • Corporate capture signals: Industry lobbied to write compliance definitions
  • No independent tribunal: Disputes resolved by political body, not judicial one
  • No public dashboard: Actions and decisions not publicly trackable in real time
  • Funding by voluntary contribution: Same flaw as UN — leverage remains with funders
  • Science advisory role only: Experts can advise but not bind decisions
  • AI governance absent: Any 2026+ framework that doesn't explicitly cover AI is already captured
The most important single indicator: who wrote the exit clause? Voluntary frameworks have clear, non-punitive withdrawal mechanisms written by neutral parties. Forced frameworks either have no exit, or an exit designed to be too costly to use. Check this first.

Historical Precedents — What Actually Worked

1945
Bretton Woods System VOLUNTARY
IMF + World Bank created pre-emptively to prevent post-war economic collapse. Worked because US had overwhelming economic dominance and made compliance the rational choice. Flaw: voting power reflected 1945 power, not 2025 reality.
1945
United Nations FORCED BY WWII
Created in direct response to catastrophe. Security Council veto built in to get major powers to join — which immediately crippled enforcement. Classic forced-governance compromise: enough to look real, not enough to work.
1957
IAEA VOLUNTARY + INSPECTIONS
Best working model for the Compact. Safeguards system with real inspectors, real verification, real consequences (NPT expulsion). Flaw: major nuclear powers exempt from same scrutiny as small states. Compact fixes this.
1994
SWIFT Financial Network TECHNICAL ENFORCEMENT
Best enforcement model. No army. No political vote required. Exclusion from SWIFT is automatic, devastating, and credible. Iran and Russia both confirm its effectiveness. The Compact's economic enforcement tier is modelled directly on this.
2020
COVID Vaccine Nationalism FORCED FAILURE
What happens when global governance is absent during a planetary crisis. Wealthy nations hoarded vaccines; poorer nations waited. Delta and Omicron variants emerged in unvaccinated populations and spread globally — proving that unequal governance is self-defeating even for those who "win" the initial hoarding race.
2026+
The Compact — Projected PROJECTED
Either emerges voluntarily from the current x-risk awareness movement + EU AI Act precedent, or is forced into existence by an event. The question is not if but which trigger creates it — and whether that trigger leaves enough functional civilisation to build something worth having.

Complexity — Honest Assessment

You asked how complex this would be. The honest answer: more complex than any single human institution ever built, less complex than the global financial system that already exists.

The global financial system — SWIFT, correspondent banking, foreign exchange clearing, derivative markets — coordinates trillions of dollars of transactions per day across 200+ jurisdictions with no central authority and no world government. It works because the architecture makes cooperation the path of least resistance and defection costly. The Compact is structurally simpler than this. The complexity is political, not technical.

The specific complexity layers:

LEGAL
Treaty Architecture
Requires a new founding treaty — not a UN amendment (too slow, veto-blocked) but a parallel instrument like the Rome Statute that creates the ICC. Minimum viable: 50 states representing 60% of global GDP ratify within 5 years. After that, economic gravity pulls others in.
TECHNICAL
Verification Infrastructure
AI compute monitoring (already technically feasible — GPU supply chains are traceable). DNA synthesis monitoring (already partially in place via BioWatch). Pathogen surveillance (WHO has infrastructure; needs authority). Nuclear — IAEA already does this. The tools exist. The mandate doesn't.
POLITICAL
Major Power Buy-In
The US and China must both join or the framework is irrelevant. Neither will join something the other controls. Solution: neither controls it — the distributed Council model with no veto is explicitly designed so both join knowing neither dominates. Self-interest, not altruism.
EQUITY
Global South Inclusion
Any framework perceived as a wealthy-nation club will be rejected and rightly so. The 15 population-elected Council seats must be weighted so that India, Nigeria, Indonesia, and Brazil have genuine power — not consultative status. Compliance benefits must visibly flow to the Global South first.

AI Design Input — What This Framework Would Recommend

You asked what AI would recommend if tasked with solving this. The answer is uncomfortable in one specific way: the framework above is technically sound. The barrier is not design — it's political will. An AI's honest addition to this document is therefore not more architecture. It's a probability-weighted implementation path.

Path A — Voluntary emergence (15% probability)

The EU AI Act creates a regulatory gravity well that pulls other jurisdictions toward compliance simply to maintain market access. This "Brussels Effect" repeats — a coalition of 30–40 states builds the Compact voluntarily, and economic gravity does the rest. Timeline: 2028–2035. Precondition: no catastrophic AI or bio event disrupts the political window.

Path B — Crisis-forced emergence (70% probability)

A sufficiently dramatic event — an AI system causing verifiable mass harm, an engineered pathogen release, a nuclear near-miss that actually detonates — creates the psychological shock that makes the politically impossible merely politically difficult. The Compact or something like it is assembled in its wake. Timeline: unknowable, but the framework must be pre-designed and ready to deploy. The reason to design it now is to have a blueprint when the moment arrives — because crisis governance assembled in panic produces the forced/captured version.

Path C — No emergence (15% probability)

The crisis that would force governance is the crisis that eliminates the capacity to govern. Engineered pandemic at sufficient lethality, AGI-level system deployed without alignment, nuclear exchange — any of these could cross the threshold from "crisis that creates governance" to "civilisational disruption that ends the option." This is why the window matters. The longer voluntary emergence is delayed, the higher the probability that Path B's triggering event is Path C's terminating event.

The AI's single most important recommendation

Pre-draft the treaty text now, in public, with the x-risk organisations as co-authors. When the crisis arrives — and it will — the political window for action is measured in weeks, not years. Governance assembled from scratch under pressure produces the UN: structurally compromised from birth. The Compact, if drafted, debated, and refined in advance, can be ratified and operational within months of a triggering event rather than decades. The design work is the most valuable thing that can be done before the crisis. After it, there is no time.
