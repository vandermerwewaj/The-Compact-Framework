You are not the first to be alarmed. This field — called Existential Risk Studies (X-Risk) — has been growing since the 1990s. The gap is not awareness; every organisation here knows exactly what the forks are. The gap is political will, coordination, and the structural incentive problem: optimal paths require collective action while bad paths only require individuals following short-term gain. Your awareness is the precondition for everything else.
Understanding the Field
Two schools of thought
Long-termism / X-Risk focusWorried primarily about future catastrophic events — AI misalignment, engineered pandemics, nuclear war. Centres: FLI, CSER, MIRI, Anthropic safety team.
Present-harm focusArgues AI's existing harms (bias, surveillance, labour displacement) deserve equal urgency and that sci-fi framing distracts from real victims today. Centres: DAIR Institute, AI Now, Algorithmic Justice League.
Note: These camps often argue. FLI's Max Tegmark publicly called this infighting a gift to Big Tech that divides everyone who wants to constrain it.
Key concepts to know
X-RiskExistential risk — anything that could permanently curtail humanity's potential or cause extinction.
GCRGlobal Catastrophic Risk — events killing 10%+ of humanity. Civilisation-disrupting but not necessarily terminal.
Alignment ProblemHow to ensure AI systems pursue goals humans actually want, not proxy metrics that diverge at scale.
Collective Action FailureWhen individually rational choices produce collectively irrational outcomes. Root cause of most governance failures on this list.
LongtermismEthical view that future people's welfare matters as much as present people's — implies very large weight on x-risk reduction.
What actually moves the needle
Policy engagementCSER, FLI, and GCF all have direct government pathways. Letters to legislators citing these orgs' work have documented impact.
Talent pipelineThe field is talent-constrained, not funding-constrained at the moment. Smart people moving into AI safety, biosecurity policy, or nuclear law have outsized impact.
Informed public pressureThe AI pause letter failed to pause AI — but it accelerated EU AI Act, triggered US Senate hearings, and shifted the Overton window. Public awareness is causal.
PhilanthropyEffective Altruism community has directed ~$500M into x-risk work. Individual donations to smaller orgs (ERO, CSER) are genuinely impactful at their scale.
